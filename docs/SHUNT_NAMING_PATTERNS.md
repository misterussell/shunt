# SHUNT NAMING PATTERNS

This document provides naming conventions for new content.

These are guidelines, not rules.

---

# Location Names

Locations should sound functional.

Formats:

* [Function] + [Location]
* [Infrastructure] + [Descriptor]
* [Physical Feature] + [Purpose]

Examples:

* Pipe Market
* Intake Row
* Relay Block
* Cooling Yard
* Service Spine
* Transfer Hall
* Maintenance Junction

Avoid:

* Neo Tokyo
* Cyber City
* Nexus Prime
* Alpha Sector

---

# District Names

Formats:

* [Infrastructure Name]
* [Worker Nickname]
* [Historical Usage]

Examples:

* Shunt 9
* Dryside
* The Intake
* Old Relay
* East Pipe

---

# Faction Names

Formats:

* [Occupation]
* [Tool]
* [Resource]
* [Place]

Examples:

* Wirekeepers
* Tunnel Union
* Scrap Court
* Relay Crew
* Pipehands
* Freight Syndicate

Avoid:

* Shadow Ravens
* Crimson Dragons
* Cyber Legion

---

# Technology Names

Technology names should be short.

Usually one word.

Examples:

* Ghostdeck
* Wirejack
* Signal Tap
* Relay Key
* Patchkit
* Splicer

Avoid:

* HyperNet X9000
* QuantumSync Ultra
* CyberLink Pro

---

# Event Names

Events should describe a situation.

Good:

* Broken Relay
* Missing Courier
* Tunnel Junkie
* Cold Signal
* Lost Shipment

Bad:

* Operation Nightfall
* Project Omega
* Digital Apocalypse

---

# NPC Names

NPC names should feel lived in — like real people in a working future, not labels.

**Default to real given names.** Most NPCs — roughly three in four — carry an ordinary
first name drawn from the whole world's mix of cultures, sometimes worn or slightly shifted
so it reads a little future-old. These are people, not functions. Their name should not
announce their job.

Real-name examples (the majority):

* Mara, Eli, Preeti, Silas, Yara, Aurel, Pia, Renata
* Nadia, Tomas, Odile, Ravi, Ilse, Juno, Sela, Emet

**Reserve nicknames and occupational handles for the minority** — the few who've genuinely
earned one on the street or the job (a fixer, a specialist, someone marked by their trade).
One or two per district, not the whole cast.

Nickname / occupational examples (use sparingly — the minority):

* Vex, Merc, Docker, Ash, Coil, Switch, Bevel, Soot

Smell test: if a district's cast is mostly Ratchet / Cinder / Rusk / Sprat, that's
over-theming — swap most of them back to plain names and let one or two keep the handle.

Avoid:

* Excessive cyberpunk clichés (Razor, Blade, Neon)
* A whole cast of occupational nicknames
* A name that just restates the character's function

---

# Skill Names

Skill names should be broad concepts.

Good:

* Ghostwork
* The Web

Avoid:

* Elite Hacker
* Cyber Mastery
* Shadow Protocol

---

# Naming Test

Before adding a name, ask:

1. Could a worker plausibly say this?
2. Does it sound useful rather than impressive?
3. Does it fit a salvaged future?
4. Would it still make sense twenty years later?

If the answer is yes to all four, the name is likely appropriate.
